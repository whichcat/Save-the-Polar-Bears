Conservation at Risk

Polar Bears Are Considered As "Vulnerable"

They are facing a high risk of extinction in the wild. Scientist estimate that theres is a 70% chance that the global population of polar bears will fall by more than a third within the next three generations.

Pollution

Polar bears in some parts of the Antarctic can carry surprisingly high loads of toxic chemicals because wind and ocean currents transport these pollutants to parts of the Antarctic, where they concentrate as they make their way up the food chain. Polar bears absorb these hiher levels when they eat seals.

Overharvesting

Unregulated commercial and sport hunting pose the gravest threat to the polar bear's future.
